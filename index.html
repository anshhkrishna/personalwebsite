inspired by the world, some things i believe:

  • “in the beginner's mind there are many possibilities, but in the expert's mind there are few.” — shunryu. beginners embrace endless possibilities; experts, constrained by knowledge, navigate a narrowed path. wisdom lies in balancing both perspectives. first principles thinking is underrated.
  • speed is everything
    • as a young person, your rate of progress is the best vector to measure growth and potential. while a lot of things in life can be thought of as chess, early on, you're almost always better off thinking of it as a race. inaction is just missed shots.
    • a lot has been said over the years about speed in the context of life, calling it a 'marathon', but every week, i re-assess my speed internally. the faster you ship, the faster you learn, the faster you refine direction.
    • a week is 2% of the year.
    • time is the denominator.
    • i need to make up for my lack of experience by my rate of learning.
  • startups
    • grow or die. fail fast.
    • learn to sell. learn to build. if you can do both, you are unstoppable.
  • people
    • be kind, be direct and transparent. life is short.
    • don't be a gatekeeper, you lose out on compounding.
    • moments are fleeting, people endure.
  • what motivates me?
    • as human beings it is our right (maybe our moral duty) to reshape the universe.
    • if you following your calling, the fatigue will be easier to bear, the disappointments will be fuel, the highs will be like nothing you've ever felt.
    • history is one long processional of crazy ideas.
    • like it or not, life is a game.
    • i like working with exceptional people who challenge and expand my worldviews. building a startup is by far the best way to do this quickly.
